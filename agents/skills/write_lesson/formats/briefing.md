# Format: Briefing

**When to use:** Orientation, announcements, curriculum structure, "how this track works" — content that informs but does not build capability. Use for ladder-level explanations, track overviews, curriculum changes, or any content where the learner mainly leaves informed, not more capable.

Not a lesson. Do not add active steps, decision rules, or failure modes unless genuinely needed. Keep it short and clear.

## Template

### 1. What this is about

1-2 sentences stating what the briefing covers and why the learner should care.

### 2. The information

Short sections, bullets, or tables. Organize for clarity, not for teaching progression. The reader is looking for information, not building a mental model.

### 3. What to do with it

If any action is needed, state it clearly. If no action is needed, omit this section entirely — don't manufacture a call to action.

## Scope

One announcement or orientation topic. If the briefing covers multiple unrelated topics, split it.

## Pre-Publish Checklist

| # | Check |
|---|-------|
| 1 | Is the information clear and actionable (if action is needed)? |
| 2 | Is it short? |
| 3 | Does it avoid pretending to be a lesson? |
