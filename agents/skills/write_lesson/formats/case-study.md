# Format: Case Study

**When to use:** The main skill is judgment — learning from a real scenario what went right, wrong, and why. Use when the user asks to "analyze," "what went wrong," or wants to teach judgment through a real or realistic scenario. The value is in the analysis, not the narrative.

## Structure Spine

### 1. Opening paragraph

The scenario and why studying it builds judgment. Lead with the transferable skill, not the story. The reader should know what kind of judgment this case study sharpens before they read the details.

### 2. The scenario

What happened, with enough detail to reason about. Include the setup, the decisions made, and the outcomes. Keep it concrete — the learner needs enough specifics to form their own analysis before reading yours.

### 3. Analysis

What went right, what went wrong, and why. This is the core of the case study. Connect specific decisions to specific outcomes. Avoid vague post-hoc narratives — show the causal chain.

### 4. Decision rule

The transferable principle the learner extracts from this case. One clear rule they can apply to their own work. Frame it as "when X, do Y because Z" — not a vague takeaway.

### 5. Try this now

Apply the judgment to a new scenario or the learner's own work. Give a concrete prompt or situation where the decision rule applies. The learner should practice the judgment, not just read about it.

## Scope

One scenario, one transferable judgment. The value is in the analysis, not the narrative. If the case study needs two unrelated analyses, it's two case studies.

## Teaching Order

Scenario -> analysis -> principle -> application.

Let the learner see what happened, understand why, extract the rule, then practice applying it.

## Pre-Publish Checklist

| # | Check |
|---|-------|
| 1 | Is the scenario concrete enough to reason about? |
| 2 | Does the analysis identify what went right and wrong? |
| 3 | Is there a transferable decision rule? |
| 4 | Is there an active step applying the judgment? |
| 5 | Is the formatting easy to scan on Discord? |
